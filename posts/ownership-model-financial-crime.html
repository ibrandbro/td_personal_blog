Who Owns What in Financial Crime Programs?

Financial crime programs often become weaker not because of policy gaps, but because ownership across Compliance, Fraud Ops, Risk, Data Engineering, and Technology is not clearly defined.

A practical model

  • Compliance defines policy, standards, governance, and escalation expectations.
  • Risk shapes methodology, thresholds, and control prioritization.
  • Fraud Ops runs alert review and frontline operational response.
  • Data Engineering enables the pipelines and data quality required for controls.
  • Technology owns platforms, integrations, workflow tooling, and stability.
